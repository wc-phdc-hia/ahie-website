Open Health Information Mediator (OpenHIM)

The Open Health Information Mediator (OpenHIM) is middleware software designed to function as the interoperability layer between different components in a health information exchange.

It provides secure communications and data governance as well as support for routing, orchestrating and translating requests as they flow between systems.

Background

The OpenHIM was initially developed by Jembi Health Systems, in collaboration with the Health Architecture Laboratory (HeAL) at the University of KwaZulu-Natal, as part of the Rwanda Health Enterprise Architecture (RHEA) project, and formed the basis for Ryan Crichton’s Master's thesis “The Open Health Information Mediator: an Architecture for Enabling Interoperability in Low to Middle Income Countries”.

It was applied in the Ramagana district in Rwanda for several years, supporting maternal, newborn and child health by brokering the information flow between the community health workers systems, the registers and the central level.

The OpenHIM has been incorporated and matured under the AHIE project as one of the core reference technologies of the OpenHIE architecture.

Application of OpenHIM

The OpenHIM technology is used in a number of settings

  • The OpenHIM is used for the Momconnect project in South Africa. This is a large mobile maternal, newborn and child health project supporting antenatal care. The OpenHIM is the interoperability piece of the stack that receives and sends messages to pregnant women and connects the data with the WEB DHIS.
  • In the Western Cape province, the OpenHIM mediates the flow of data from Premis (a primary healthcare system used by CoCT) to the DHIS2 application.
  • As part of the AHIE project, the OpenHIM mediates the flow of data between certain community healthcare systems and the PHDC main database system, as well as the SPV system.
  • In several other countries in Africa the OpenHIM software has been implemented as open source software.
