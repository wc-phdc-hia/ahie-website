  4. Open Information Health Platform (OpenIHP)

The OpenIHP platform consolidates all patient information available for a single individual in a manner that approximates an electronic health record, allowing clinicians access to longitudinal clinical data, as well as direct data input capability into the system.

Utilising the technology architecture design of the AHIE project, OpenIHP is able to provide much of the continuity-of-care functionality often associated with electronic medical records, while avoiding the challenges associated with maintaining connected point-of-care medical record systems.

To achieve this, the OpenIHP platform combines laboratory, pharmacy, discharge and clerical service information through a viewer, which incorporates intelligent linkage and data processing, driven by embedded clinical concepts.

Not only is it a platform that includes the consolidation, viewing and ability to interact with the patient data directly, the OpenIHP application also offers a rich reporting layer.

Background

The availability of personal level data from various health administrative systems (eg NHLS, pharmacy dispensing) made it possible to create a fairly rich personal shared health record, in the absence of an electronic health record.

The Single Patient Viewer was initially developed to provide a web-based accessible viewer to harness these data however, as the application evolved, it became apparent that it was possible to bolt-on functionality that would allow health care workers to capture data directly into the system.

With the advantage of having access to many of the data points required for patient care, the project could focus on the additional data elements that hadn’t already been consolidated.

The Single Patient Viewer, which was already a reference application, became a much richer platform that included direct bidirectional engagement from practitioners. It was subsequently rebranded as the Open Integrated Health Platform (OpenIHP).

The following use case was designed to establish the feasibility of patient-level data exchange between the PHDC and OpenIHP / SPV:

PHDC - OpenIHP/SPV interoperability

The prototype SPV would be matured within the AHIE project as a web-based clinical application for use in clinical areas, consolidating all patient data for any given individual across all the data domains and providing a longitudinal clinical history spanning the entire health jurisdiction.

This would include graphical views (initially for HIV, TB and diabetes) consolidating the episode, encounter, laboratory and pharmacy data in a clinically meaningful way.

The SPV would communicate with the data centre via HL7 messaging so that it could be used in other environments based on different health exchange technology and component systems.
