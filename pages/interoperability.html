Interoperability

The AHIE project design includes interoperability middleware and standardised protocols, utilised to facilitate data exchange between heterogeneous health information systems.

These interoperability layers provide secure communication between systems as well as data governance, while providing support for routing, orchestrating and translating requests as they flow between systems.

The AHIE architecture leverages the OpenHIM and OpenLHIM middleware for this purpose.

As part of the project, the OpenHIM health information mediator was matured to include appropriate HL7 endpoints for the identified data interchange scenarios. The OpenLHIM mediator was developed as an adaptation of the OpenHIM, for localised facility-based applications.

The resulting standards-based mediators and adapters developed for the OpenHIM and OpenLHIM are openly available through a mediator repository.

The Open Health Information Mediator (OpenHIM)

The OpenHIM largely serves as an interoperability layer between different components that have been developed and matured as part of the AHIE project. It is globally maintained by Jembi Health Systems, as a key partner in the AHIE project.

The Open Local Health Information Mediator (OpenLHIM)

The OpenHIM was specifically adapted to provide a flexible new service which would allow clinical data from the data centre to be linked to local facility-based applications that were not part of WAN implementations. This service is termed the Open Local Health Information Mediator, or OpenLHIM, and is a conceptual enhancement to the generic health exchange architecture. It can be considered to be the “facility corollary” of the OpenHIM.
