Open Local Health Information Mediator (OpenLHIM)

The OpenLHIM is a piece of software designed to be implemented at a local level, and works in conjunction with a point of service application to broker the flow of information from that point of service application to a central data store.

Initially the OpenLHIM data flow was unidirectional, catering specifically for the unidirectional flow of laboratory information from a database of centrally held lab information, into a local register or patient record system. It was then extended to cater for a bidirectional flow of information between the local patient data storage system and the central data storage system.

The OpenLHIM is essentially a local version of the OpenHIM which allows clinical data from the data centre to be linked to local facility-based applications that are not part of a WAN implementation.

Background

The Open Local Health Information Mediator is an innovation that was developed by Jembi and matured as part of the AHIE project, in a collaborative effort between Jembi and UCT School of Public Health and Family Medicine.

Initially it started out facilitating the flow of laboratory data from the main PHDC database to local instances of TIER.Net. It has since been expanded to include more extensive patient record information, which is bidirectionally synced with the PHDC.
