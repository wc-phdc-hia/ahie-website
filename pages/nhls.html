National Health Laboratory Service (NHLS)

South Africa is in the unique position of having a single national laboratory service (South African National Health Laboratory Services or NHLS) with a sophisticated centralised laboratory information system (Intersystems TrakCare Lab).

Under the AHIE project the interoperability functionality between the NHLS and the selected reference implementations was matured to enable data exchange for direct online as well as intermittant connectivity scenarios.

The following use cases were designed to establish the feasibility of patient-level data exchange with the NHLS:

HPRS – NHLS interoperability

The specific use case selected to test this was integration of HPRS with the NHLS to alert clerks to the availability of laboratory results on patient arrival and to handle the hand-off to the appropriate laboratory results portal. Once this functionality was matured in HPRS, it could provide the basis for further lookups for clinical data from other repositories. In addition, alerting functionality could be added for additional events, such as for patients who were lost-to-care elsewhere on the platform, especially for conditions with transmission risk, such as TB and drug-resistant TB.

TIER.Net – NHLS interoperability

To improve the fidelity of facility-derived operational and patient management reports, laboratory results were critically needed in local instances of TIER.Net. The reports would identify patients who required interventions, such as adherence interventions where viral loads were persistently elevated, or retention or re-engagement in care for patients who had missed appointments or disengaged from TB or HIV care. It was anticipated that TIER.Net – NHLS interoperability, whether it was direct (in the case of facilities that were online) or via the HIE would help mitigate one of the significant weaknesses of facility-based HIV and TB data, which was the failure to file, record and capture laboratory results. Interoperability for this reference implementation would be via the HIE.
