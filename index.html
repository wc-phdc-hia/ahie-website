The Reference African Health Information Exchange (AHIE) promotes the optimisation of patient healthcare through a replicable, context-appropriate health information exchange design.

With a focus on person-level data, the design enables data capture as well as cascade reporting for individuals throughout their journey of disease management and care at clinician level, while allowing for aggregated data availability at a regional and national healthcare management level.

Key to the AHIE design is patient-level data harmonisation, data exchange and bi-directional interoperability between a set of matured health information technologies.

Components

The components of the AHIE project form part of an architectural model designed to promote the exchange of patient-level health information across disparate applications.

Key components identified include interoperability technologies, data harmonisation strategies and patient-level healthcare management applications.

Unique Patient Identifier
Uniquely identifies the patient across all linked healthcare systems (HPRS / PMI).
Health Data Centre
A central repository of patient-level data with data harmonisation functionality.
Open Interoperability Layer
Middleware designed to allow bi-directional data exchange between applications.
Healthcare Application Implementations
Enabling patient-level disease management and reporting.

Interoperability

Data exchange between heterogeneous health information systems is made possible via interoperability middleware and standardised protocols.

The interoperability middleware provides secure communication between systems as well as data governance, while providing support for routing, orchestrating and translating requests as they flow between systems.

The AHIE architecture leverages the OpenHIM and OpenLHIM middleware for this purpose.

Open Health Information Mediator (OpenHIM)
Middleware designed to ease interoperability between disparate information systems.
Open Local Health Information Mediator (OpenLHIM)
Middleware designed to allow interoperability between facility-based software.

Implementations

Various applications, matured or developed under the AHIE project, have been incorporated into the architectural design.

These implementations work together to allow the bi-directional flow of patient-level health information across heterogeneous applications and enable longitudinal disease management and reporting.

OpenIHP / SPV
Patient-level data capture with longitudinal disease management and reporting.
National Health Laboratory Service
Laboratory test results made available to applications via the NHLS.
TIER.Net
An application designed specifically to manage HIV, TB and Maternal healthcare.
Other
Additional applications included in the project.
